Data Processing Agreement (DPA)

Last Updated: December 8, 2025 | Effective Date: January 1, 2026

Note: This DPA applies to Enterprise customers and is mandatory for EU, Canadian, and regulated data processing. Free and Professional users accept it by using the platform.

1. Definitions 2. Scope & Applicability 3. Data Controller vs. Processor 4. Processing Instructions 5. Sub-processors 6. Data Subject Rights 7. Security Measures 8. International Transfers 9. Incident Response & Breach Notification 10. Deletion & Return of Data

1. Definitions

  • Controller: The natural or legal person who determines the purposes and means of processing (typically you, the customer)
  • Processor: The entity (Aidni Global LLP / Nexora Suite) that processes data on behalf of the controller
  • Processing: Any operation performed on data (collection, storage, use, analysis, deletion)
  • Personal Data: Information relating to an identified or identifiable natural person
  • Data Subject: The individual to whom personal data relates
  • Breach: Unauthorized access, loss, alteration, or disclosure of personal data

2. Scope & Applicability

A. Applicable Laws:
  • GDPR (EU General Data Protection Regulation)
  • PIPEDA (Canadian Personal Information Protection & Electronic Documents Act)
  • CCPA (California Consumer Privacy Act)
  • Uruguayan Data Protection Law #18.600
  • India Information Technology Act & Rules
B. This DPA Applies When:
  • You process personal data of EU residents (GDPR applies)
  • You process personal data of Canadian residents (PIPEDA applies)
  • You process personal data of California residents (CCPA applies)
  • You are an Enterprise customer (mandatory)
  • You request a signed DPA as a condition of use
C. Data Processing Categories:
  • Customer Data: Business information you upload (invoices, customer records, transactions)
  • User Data: Information about your team members (names, emails, roles)
  • Usage Data: Log data, analytics, system metrics

3. Data Controller vs. Processor

A. You Are the Controller:
  • You determine what personal data to process
  • You decide how and why data is processed
  • You are responsible for lawful basis and consent
  • You remain liable for GDPR/PIPEDA compliance
B. We Are the Processor:
  • We process data only on your documented instructions
  • We do not use your data for our own purposes (except service provision)
  • We implement safeguards required by law
  • We assist you in fulfilling data subject rights
C. Your Responsibilities:
  • Obtain lawful basis for processing (consent, contract, legitimate interest, etc.)
  • Obtain appropriate consent from data subjects where required
  • Provide privacy notices to data subjects
  • Respond to data subject access requests
  • Notify authorities of breaches if legally required

4. Processing Instructions

A. Processing Activities Authorized:
  • Storage and management of your business data
  • Processing to provide platform features
  • Analytics and usage reporting (anonymized)
  • Security monitoring and fraud detection
  • System backups and disaster recovery
  • API and integration processing per your configuration
B. Prohibited Processing:
  • We will not use your data for marketing our services (except anonymized case studies with permission)
  • We will not share data with third parties except sub-processors
  • We will not combine your data with other customers' data
  • We will not use data for training AI/ML models without consent
C. Additional Processing Requests:

If you require additional processing activities, email support@aidniglobal.in. We will evaluate and either approve or decline in writing.

B. Your Approval Rights:
  • Enterprise customers have the right to review sub-processor lists
  • You may object to new sub-processors within 30 days
  • If you object, we work with you to find alternatives or you may terminate with 30 days' notice
  • Free/Professional users accept our standard sub-processors by using the service
C. Sub-processor Agreements:
  • All sub-processors sign Data Processing Agreements
  • Sub-processors have access only to data necessary for their service
  • We remain liable for sub-processor compliance

6. Data Subject Rights

A. Rights We Support (GDPR/PIPEDA):
  • Access: We provide tools to export your data; we respond to formal requests within 30 days
  • Rectification: You can update data directly; for archived data, contact support
  • Erasure: We delete data upon your request, subject to legal retention requirements
  • Restrict Processing: We can restrict processing of specific data upon request
  • Portability: We provide data in machine-readable format (CSV, JSON)
  • Objection: You may object to marketing use; we stop immediately
B. Your Obligation to Support Rights:
  • You must provide your data subjects a way to request rights from you
  • You forward requests to us at support@aidniglobal.in with appropriate documentation
  • We will assist you within the timeframe required by law (typically 30 days)
  • Enterprise customers get dedicated support for complex requests
C. Automated Decision-Making:
  • Nexora Suite does not perform automated decision-making that produces legal effects
  • Our fraud detection and abuse prevention use automated rules, but do not make final decisions
  • You retain full control over business decisions

7. Security Measures

A. Technical Safeguards:
  • Encryption in Transit: TLS 1.3 for all communications
  • Encryption at Rest: AES-256 encryption for stored data
  • Access Control: Role-based access control (RBAC) with principle of least privilege
  • Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA): Optional for users, mandatory for admins
  • Logging & Monitoring: All access logged, 24/7 security monitoring
  • Intrusion Detection: Automated threat detection and response
B. Organizational Safeguards:
  • Staff Training: All employees trained on data protection and privacy
  • NDA Agreements: All staff sign strict confidentiality agreements
  • Access Restrictions: Only authorized personnel access customer data
  • Vendor Management: Regular audits of sub-processors
C. Physical Safeguards:
  • Data centers with 24/7 security and surveillance
  • Biometric access controls to server rooms
  • Fire detection and suppression systems
  • Redundant power supplies and HVAC
  • ISO 27001 certified data centers
D. Compliance Certifications:
  • ISO/IEC 27001 (Information Security Management)
  • SOC 2 Type II (Security, Availability, Confidentiality)
  • GDPR compliance audit (annual)
  • Penetration testing (quarterly)

8. International Data Transfers

A. Data Location:
  • Data is primarily stored in AWS data centers in the US and EU
  • Backups are replicated across multiple regions for redundancy
  • Enterprise customers can request specific region storage
B. EU Data Transfers:
  • For EU personal data, we rely on Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs)
  • Our AWS processor uses EU-US Standard Contractual Clauses
  • We maintain adequate safeguards for post-Schrems II compliance
  • We can provide data localization (EU-only storage) upon request for Enterprise customers
C. Adequacy Decisions:
  • We rely on Standard Contractual Clauses for transfers to countries without adequacy decisions
  • We regularly review legal developments affecting transfers
  • We will notify Enterprise customers of material changes

9. Incident Response & Breach Notification

A. Security Incident Definition:

A security incident is any unauthorized or accidental event involving:

  • Unauthorized access or disclosure of data
  • Loss, corruption, or alteration of data
  • System outage affecting data confidentiality, integrity, or availability
B. Our Response Obligations:
  • Immediate: We investigate and contain the incident
  • 24 hours: We notify Enterprise customers of confirmed breaches
  • 72 hours: We notify Professional subscribers (GDPR requirement)
  • Notification Details: Nature of breach, data affected, scope, remediation steps
  • Cooperation: We assist with regulatory notifications and data subject communications
C. Your Notification Obligations:
  • You are responsible for notifying data subjects and regulators if required
  • We provide information to assist your notifications
  • You determine risk level and notification necessity based on your circumstances
D. Prevention Measures:
  • Regular security audits and penetration testing
  • Automated vulnerability scanning
  • Incident response plan (tested quarterly)
  • Cyber insurance coverage

10. Deletion & Return of Data

A. Data Deletion Upon Termination:
  • You can export your data anytime
  • Upon account cancellation, we retain data per your subscription tier:
    • Free users: 90 days
    • Professional: 30 days
    • Enterprise: per custom agreement
  • After retention period, all data is permanently deleted
  • Deleted data cannot be recovered
B. Data in Backups:
  • Backups are also subject to the same deletion timeline
  • Backup deletion may take up to 90 days due to retention policies
  • Enterprise customers can request expedited deletion
C. Legal Hold Exception:
  • We may retain data longer if required by law (e.g., court order, tax law)
  • We will notify you of legal holds and provide timeline
D. Certification of Deletion:
  • Enterprise customers can request written certification of data deletion
  • Certification provided within 30 days of deletion
